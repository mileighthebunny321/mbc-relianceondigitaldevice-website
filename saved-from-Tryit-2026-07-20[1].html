The Problem

Laptops are valuable learning tools, but education and psychology research suggests that excessive reliance on digital devices can reduce face-to-face interaction, increase opportunities for distraction, and contribute to longer periods of sedentary behaviour.

  • Reduced social interaction: Face-to-face conversations help students build communication, empathy, teamwork and friendships. When everyone focuses on their own screen, opportunities for collaboration may decrease.
  • Digital distractions: Notifications, games and social media can interrupt concentration. Research has found that task-switching between schoolwork and entertainment can reduce learning efficiency and attention.
  • Physical inactivity: Long periods sitting behind a laptop can reduce movement throughout the school day, which may affect wellbeing, posture and energy levels.
  • Learning engagement: Collaborative discussions and hands-on classroom activities often improve participation, confidence and deeper understanding of concepts.
  • Balance is important: Technology is most effective when combined with active discussion, practical learning and opportunities for students to interact with one another.

These findings suggest that schools can benefit from encouraging a healthy balance between digital learning and face-to-face collaboration rather than relying exclusively on laptops throughout the day.

Our Solutions

📚 Laptop-Free Collaborative Lessons

Introduce selected lessons where students work in small groups using discussion, whiteboards or printed resources to encourage communication and teamwork.

🎯 Stronger Classroom Focus

Teachers can establish clear expectations for when laptops should be open and when they should be closed, reducing opportunities for off-task gaming and social media use.

🏃 Active Learning Activities

Include movement-based learning, practical experiments and collaborative classroom challenges to reduce sedentary time and increase engagement.

💬 Digital Wellbeing Education

Teach students strategies for managing distractions, notifications and screen time so they develop lifelong healthy technology habits.

🤝 Balanced Technology Use

Continue using laptops where they clearly enhance learning while intentionally including opportunities for face-to-face collaboration and discussion throughout the school day.
